Hardware

I love making things. I Particularly enjoy making things which:

  • 🔌 are electronic
  • 🔊 have something to do with audio
  • 🎛 are interactive
  • 📽 are somewhat esoteric
  • ⚗ include some kind of experimentation, exploration or learning curve

The Google OpenNSynthSuper is a great example of a project that really inspired me.

The OpenNSynthSuper is an open-source design and software, DIY, neural-network sound-morphing synthesizer.

The hardware is built on top of a Raspberry Pi, essentialy like a very large 'hat'.

The PCB incorporates a capacitive 'touch' pad, and a series of potentiometers that interface with a little STM32F0 processor.

The STM32F0 interfaces with the raspberry pi, which is the main synth engine.

The audio output is handled by a TI DAC.

Given enough time, I would like to make a eurorack conversion of this project, that includes CV control over the 6 potentiometers.
